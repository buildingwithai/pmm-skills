# 90-Day Launch Roadmap

**Purpose:** Plan and execute a structured product launch from initial planning through post-launch optimization.

**When to use:** For Tier 1 and Tier 2 launches that require comprehensive planning and coordination.

**Output:** Complete timeline with phases, milestones, and accountabilities.

---

## Overview

The 90-day roadmap provides a proven structure for product launches, broken into three 30-day phases. This framework is based on the "Ready-Set-Go" model used by leading product teams.

**Timeline:** 90 days from kickoff to Day 30 post-launch

**Applies to:** Tier 1 and Tier 2 launches (Tier 3 uses simplified version)

---

## The Ready-Set-Go Framework

### Phase 1: READY (Days 1-30) - Foundation

**Goal:** Build the strategic foundation for launch success

**Key activities:**
- Customer discovery and validation
- Competitive analysis and positioning
- Launch tier determination and resource allocation
- Cross-functional team formation
- Success metrics definition

**Deliverables:**
- Positioning document (from `positioning` skill)
- Competitive landscape analysis
- Launch brief and stakeholder alignment
- RACI matrix
- Success metrics dashboard

---

### Phase 2: SET (Days 31-60) - Build Momentum

**Goal:** Create launch assets and build pre-launch buzz

**Key activities:**
- Messaging and content creation
- Beta testing and feedback integration
- Sales enablement materials
- Pre-launch audience building
- Channel strategy execution

**Deliverables:**
- Messaging framework (from `messaging` skill)
- Sales battlecards and training
- Marketing content (blog posts, videos, landing pages)
- Beta user feedback and product iterations
- Pre-launch email list or waitlist

---

### Phase 3: GO (Days 61-90) - Launch & Iterate

**Goal:** Execute launch and optimize based on real data

**Key activities:**
- Multi-channel launch execution
- Real-time monitoring and response
- Post-launch feedback collection
- Rapid iteration on messaging/product
- Scale what works, kill what doesn't

**Deliverables:**
- Launch day execution
- Week 1 performance report
- Iteration roadmap
- Launch retrospective
- Growth playbook for next 90 days

---

## Detailed Day-by-Day Breakdown

### Days 1-30: READY (Foundation)

#### Week 1 (Days 1-7): Discovery & Alignment

**Days 1-2: Kickoff**
- Launch team kickoff meeting
- Review product roadmap and launch scope
- Confirm tier classification (use `01-launch-tier-framework.md`)
- Align on success metrics and OKRs

**Days 3-5: Customer Discovery**
- Interview 5-10 target customers (if new product)
- Validate problem/solution fit
- Understand jobs-to-be-done
- Identify key use cases

**Days 6-7: Competitive Analysis**
- Map competitive landscape
- Identify differentiation opportunities
- Document competitive alternatives
- Begin positioning work (use `positioning` skill)

**Week 1 Deliverables:**
- Launch brief (1-pager)
- Customer discovery insights
- Competitive landscape map

---

#### Week 2 (Days 8-14): Positioning & Strategy

**Days 8-10: Positioning Workshop**
- Run April Dunford positioning workshop
- Define best-fit customers
- Articulate unique value
- Determine market category
- Complete positioning canvas

**Days 11-12: GTM Strategy**
- Decide: Product-led vs sales-led motion
- Select target channels
- Determine budget allocation
- Create high-level GTM plan

**Days 13-14: Resource Planning**
- Build RACI matrix (see `04-cross-functional-coordination.md`)
- Assign owners for each deliverable
- Identify resource gaps
- Confirm timeline feasibility

**Week 2 Deliverables:**
- Completed positioning document
- GTM strategy one-pager
- RACI matrix
- Resource allocation plan

---

#### Week 3 (Days 15-21): Messaging & Planning

**Days 15-17: Messaging Development**
- Create Pain-Claim-Gain messaging (use `messaging` skill)
- Adapt for multiple audiences (P1/P2/P3)
- Develop message architecture
- Write core value propositions

**Days 18-19: Success Metrics**
- Define AARRR metrics for launch
- Set Week 1, Month 1, Quarter 1 targets
- Build analytics tracking plan
- Create dashboard templates

**Days 20-21: Detailed Planning**
- Build detailed launch plan (Days 31-90)
- Assign deadlines for all deliverables
- Identify dependencies and risks
- Schedule all key meetings/milestones

**Week 3 Deliverables:**
- Messaging framework
- Success metrics dashboard
- Detailed launch plan (31-day view)

---

#### Week 4 (Days 22-30): Beta Preparation

**Days 22-24: Beta Program Design**
- Define beta goals (feedback, validation, testimonials)
- Recruit beta participants (use existing customers or prospects)
- Create beta feedback mechanisms
- Build beta onboarding materials

**Days 25-27: Content Planning**
- Outline all launch content needs
- Assign content creation to owners
- Set content deadlines (need 2 weeks before launch)
- Plan content distribution strategy

**Days 28-30: Sales Enablement Prep**
- Begin battlecard drafting
- Outline training materials
- Schedule sales enablement sessions
- Create demo environment plan

**Week 4 Deliverables:**
- Beta program plan
- Content calendar
- Sales enablement outline

**CHECKPOINT:** End of Day 30 review
- Is positioning clear and validated?
- Is messaging resonating with target audience?
- Are resources allocated and committed?
- Are we on track for beta launch?

---

### Days 31-60: SET (Build Momentum)

#### Week 5 (Days 31-37): Beta Launch

**Days 31-32: Beta Kickoff**
- Launch to beta participants
- Send onboarding emails
- Activate support channels
- Begin monitoring feedback

**Days 33-35: Beta Monitoring**
- Daily check-ins with beta users
- Collect feedback (surveys, interviews, analytics)
- Identify bugs and UX issues
- Track activation and engagement

**Days 36-37: Product Iteration**
- Prioritize beta feedback
- Make critical product improvements
- Update documentation
- Prepare for wider rollout if needed

**Week 5 Deliverables:**
- Beta user feedback report
- Product iteration plan
- Early testimonials or quotes

---

#### Week 6 (Days 38-44): Content Creation

**Days 38-40: Core Content**
- Write launch blog post
- Create product video (demo or explainer)
- Design landing page
- Write email sequences (pre-launch, launch, post-launch)

**Days 41-43: Channel-Specific Content**
- LinkedIn posts and video
- Product Hunt submission page
- Reddit posts (authentic, value-first)
- Twitter/X thread
- Press release (if Tier 1)

**Days 44: Content Review**
- Review all content with stakeholders
- Test messaging with customers (use Wynter if budget allows)
- Iterate based on feedback
- Finalize for approval

**Week 6 Deliverables:**
- All core launch content completed
- Channel-specific content ready
- Content approved by stakeholders

---

#### Week 7 (Days 45-51): Sales Enablement

**Days 45-47: Training Materials**
- Complete sales battlecards (product, competitor, objection)
- Create discovery question guide
- Build demo script and environment
- Develop pricing/packaging overview

**Days 48-49: Sales Training**
- Run sales enablement sessions
- Demo the product
- Role-play discovery calls
- Address objections and questions

**Days 50-51: Sales Activation**
- Distribute all enablement materials
- Set up CRM for tracking launch leads
- Create sales plays for launch period
- Align on launch day support

**Week 7 Deliverables:**
- Complete sales enablement package
- Trained sales team
- Sales plays activated

---

#### Week 8 (Days 52-60): Pre-Launch Buzz

**Days 52-54: Audience Building**
- Launch waitlist or early access program
- Activate email sequences
- Begin community engagement (Product Hunt, Reddit, LinkedIn)
- Tease product on social media

**Days 55-57: Partner/Influencer Outreach**
- Brief partners on launch
- Reach out to micro-influencers
- Coordinate co-marketing activities
- Secure launch day amplification

**Days 58-60: Final Preparations**
- Complete launch day playbook (see `06-launch-day-playbook.md`)
- Assign war room roles
- Test all systems (landing pages, analytics, support)
- Run launch day dry run

**Week 8 Deliverables:**
- Waitlist or early access group built
- Partner/influencer commitments secured
- Launch day playbook ready
- All systems tested

**CHECKPOINT:** End of Day 60 review
- Is all content ready and approved?
- Is sales team trained and confident?
- Are launch day roles and responsibilities clear?
- Is pre-launch buzz building?

---

## Compressed Timeline Options

### Tier 2: 60-Day Compressed Roadmap

For Tier 2 launches, compress to 60 days:

**Days 1-20: READY**
- Week 1: Discovery & positioning (compressed to 7 days)
- Week 2: Messaging & GTM strategy
- Week 3: Beta prep & content planning

**Days 21-45: SET**
- Week 4: Beta launch & feedback
- Week 5: Content creation & sales enablement
- Week 6: Pre-launch buzz & final prep

**Days 46-60: GO**
- Day 46: Launch
- Week 7: Rapid iteration
- Week 8-9: Optimize & scale

**What to cut:**
- Extensive customer discovery (use existing data)
- Long beta period (2 weeks vs 4 weeks)
- Some content pieces (focus on core)
- Reduced pre-launch buzz period

---

### Solopreneur: 45-Day Sprint

For solopreneurs, compress further to 45 days:

**Days 1-15: READY**
- Days 1-5: Positioning & messaging (use AI tools)
- Days 6-10: Content creation (leverage templates)
- Days 11-15: Beta with 5-10 users

**Days 16-35: SET**
- Days 16-25: Refine product & content
- Days 26-30: Build waitlist
- Days 31-35: Pre-launch on Product Hunt, IndieHackers

**Days 36-45: GO**
- Day 36: Launch on Product Hunt
- Days 37-40: Engage and respond
- Days 41-45: Iterate and optimize

**Solo advantages:**
- Faster decision-making
- No stakeholder approvals needed
- Can pivot quickly
- Lean on community vs paid marketing

## Common Timeline Mistakes

### Mistake 1: Starting Too Late

**Problem:** Beginning 90-day plan only 60 days before desired launch date

**Impact:** Rushed execution, poor quality, team burnout

**Fix:** Launch date should be 90 days FROM start of planning, not FROM today

---

### Mistake 2: Compressing Beta Phase

**Problem:** Beta testing for only 1 week to save time

**Impact:** Major bugs discovered after launch, poor user experience

**Fix:** Minimum 2-week beta for Tier 2, 3-4 weeks for Tier 1

---

### Mistake 3: Content Created at Last Minute

**Problem:** Blog posts and videos created in final week

**Impact:** Poor quality, no time for revisions, stakeholder conflicts

**Fix:** All content should be complete 2 weeks before launch

---

### Mistake 4: No Buffer Time

**Problem:** Every day scheduled back-to-back with no slack

**Impact:** One delay cascades into everything else

**Fix:** Build 10-15% buffer time, especially around dependencies

## Adapting for Different Contexts

### B2B SaaS Launch
- **Longer sales cycles:** Allow for 6-12 month sales cycles post-launch
- **Enterprise beta:** Involve design partners early (3-6 months before launch)
- **Procurement:** Factor in security reviews, legal, procurement timelines

---

### Consumer Product Launch
- **Faster cycles:** Can compress timeline to 45-60 days
- **Viral mechanics:** Focus heavily on referral loops and sharing
- **App stores:** Add 1-2 weeks for Apple/Google review processes

---

### Developer Tools
- **Documentation heavy:** Allocate more time for API docs, SDKs
- **Community first:** Build on GitHub, Stack Overflow, Hacker News
- **Open beta:** Consider public beta on GitHub
